Schitzo’s Notes

For edgerunners, combat notes, cyberware and Smasher class phycos.

Before we get started, I want to tell you schizophrenia sucks. And it’s not just seeing or hearing things, it can be a pervasive paranoia about the world, yourself, or god knows what else. Who the fuck knows who causes it, but it melts your brain into delusion and madness.

It can happen with the onset of cyberpsychosis commonly, you will hear people or things talk to you, usually but not consistently your cyberware, giving you commands in accordance with their use. Sometimes you get a spider rig, and then suddenly work until your body is digested in the soup of your work, or, well, we’ve all seen “blood baths”.

        Ok maybe you haven’t, and maybe as a lawman I’ve seen too much, these are all fair. But the point I’m trying to make is schizophrenia, my curse, is far more serious than my name or my stories imply. However, to get help with cyberpsychosis, you will probably have to deal with it’s label, and if you’re unlucky like I am, it’s nickname.

        That isn’t to say don’t get help. For example, I’m currently in extensive cyberpsychosis therapy for the second time in my life, and due to my long service as a lawman, and being seen as both senile and a minor threat to corpos. My precinct is paying for me to retire, as long as I get my cyber swapped for medical grade. Oh and never call them again.

Anyways, I’ve had a revelation. Unlike my usual schizophrenic episodes, I feel like I’ve waken up from a dream. I’ve begun forming a system for describing how the world works, based on stats, and skills. Will have a document that explains ppl later. But first, advice for edgerunners and the gods of this land.

But you want some advice don’t you?

        Well you don’t get called unkillable for nothing. I’ve been a lawman ever since I got booted from militech, and here are the most effective strategies edgerunners, boostergangers, and corporate folks (if you were wondering why no admin likes me) have used to kill me. For the record, none have succeeded, though many have won.

I have the title unkillable not for surviving, but for being grossly mangled and surviving.

Tactics

Good Tactics are a skill you must learn, namely, before you end up in combat. But during can always work. In short, talk to experienced solos, find out what works for them. But here’s what I know:

Team composition

On overlap: A little bit of overlap is good, from what I’ve seen, you can even make two med-techs work.

        See, every team has it’s specialty, that’s often good to keep the team’s cohesion. Because you can end up in a team of mostly net-runners. While that can make you bad at combat, you know for a fact that you’re going to steal digitally, and you're always going to go through the net. That kind of cohesion can help that group.

And, even if it’s just netrunners, that can even help them keep cohesion in combat. Given a signal to retreat doesn’t need to be given. And also makes them a royal pain to prosecute. So be warned, if an experienced lawman sees you, the entire fucking department will see you as a severe annoyance.

Leaders however: More than two fixers, or execs doesn’t always necessarily end well. These kinds of folks don’t really like each other, and can’t really be bargained with.

Rockerboys: Leaders of your most fun gigs. They can sometimes lead together, but will often butt heads.  

But not all the people in their bands are rockerboys. Rockerboys, classically, are only the face of the band. They aren’t necessarily the best players. For example, one of my favorite musicians, Rhymfest, is a good rockerboy, despite how much his material is loved, or his ability to produce and make music is legendary. He always ends up a tech to Kanye.

Medtech requirement: You will need a med tech that you trust. However, they don’t need to fight with you. You will only need someone who is good with first aid.

        For example, as a lawman, I go to the city hospital, and know everyone well. Also, because a lot of my compatriots either die or get promoted beyond me, I have skill in first aid and some paramedic techniques.

        But that can be inconvenient. When I made the switch from corpo to lawman, I was between medtechs for weeks, and in all likelihood, shouldn’t have survived. So, if you can get a medtech, do.

Solo requirement: Solos can fight better than anyone. however, when it comes to combat, a solid plan, avoiding combat and a medtech can replace them, For an experienced team. For an inexperienced team, get a solo.

Other things you should do before combat

Paranoia: Congratulations this is how schizophrenia can manifest. But seriously it’s very helpful.

        No fixer cares about you. Not even Woodchipper. No exec will ever care more about people than money. Or they’d be a rockerboy, Solo, or… you know what you get the point.

keep an eye over your shoulder, I know solos are best at it, but rockerboys, execs, and medias should keep an ear to the ground if anyone wants them dead. And, let’s be real here, fixers, you know a guy who can find out who’s coming after you, don’t short the cash for once.

Gear: Get the best gear you can. Steal for it, kill for it, nothing will have you live quite like gear. Oh, and to buy it, debt is good. As long as you have a plan to clear it within the month.

        Back when I worked for militech, if I intended to do low end pr work (also known as lynchings) for militech, I would mostly stick to food that month. But, if I was finally healed after the last stupid mission. It was a cyberarm (mine are all medical now), a popup grenade launcher, and the tyger claws would know the color of sky in their streets.

What probably got you in this situation and how to avoid it.

Debt: Debt can also be very bad. You do not want to have debt in-between jobs, and if you think it might happen, line up another job.

        But, monetary debt is not the only kind that you don’t want to have. If you are indebted to a person. They are probably far more willing to kill you.

        And if you ever wonder why your life sucks, ask yourself, who feels like you owe them? Namely if they feel like you’ve fucked them. If it’s your bosses, that’s probably why you have more kills than most cybersquad folks, and are still a street cop.

        Sadly, this is very simple, and there’s next to no way to dodge it, you just have to be nice and pay in full.

Fixers or execs: Everyone needs one. But don’t expect them to fight by your side.

They say, the best allegory for the monsters in elfland's online is cyberphycos. And that may be true. But villains? These are them. Nothing can fuck you over quite like a fixer. And no one has the resources to ruin your life like an exec.

These people will know you’re going to get shot way before you do. So, be the person they hire to shoot, or, tell them you’re going to shoot someone to make their job easier.

You’d be surprised how much credit you can get with a fixer who knows you’ll tell them if you’ll kill someone.

Cults and boostergangs: If they aren’t fighting now expect it.

        This also includes religions, but, the difference between a cult and a religion is your propensity to die while worshiping. Religions fight other religions for mental space. And cults fight violently for mental space.

        Gangs are simmilar, but fight for territory, or sometimes market space if they’re drug dealers. For the record, the drug dealers are as black hearted as corpos. Never trust them. Despite always being more fun.

Now, occasionally you can expect them to fight for you, if you do something for them. But take it from a lawman, who has seen this deal go south more times than I can count. Don’t do that in the first place.

        Avoid them, always. Do not spit on market preachers; they will know a cyberpsycho named Jesus who will tear you a new eye socket. There’s three in my precinct.

Lawmen: You got in trouble with the law? Well it happens to everyone. Seriously, I saw it, and had to start letting petty thieves go.

        But yeah so you broke the law, everyone does, even lawmen. The corpos do it the most with corruption and what not, but, we can always be bought or convinced otherwise.

        Seriously. Lawmen, such as myself, are cowards, and would rather either A, make money, or B, attempt to make the world better by talking things out.

        Well the low ranking cops want that. C-squad will just kill you, and you won’t be able to talk to our execs, but stay a low-priority problem and we’ll walk.

So you didn’t listen to me

Don’t worry, it happens all the time. Hell, I don’t sometimes. What can I say, I see lone  corpos, it’s very hard for me to not shoot them. So here’s basically how tactics work

Cover: Also known as Ranged combat. Because if you aren’t using cover in ranged combat you’re doomed. So, thing about cover, you have to put all of your effort into popping out and in to shoot without being shot.

If you forget to move back into cover after you shoot, you will be shot. If you are not fast enough to move back into cover, you will be shot. Thanks to the nature of cover and intelligent edge runners, you will be shot period.

To deal with cover, buy and use grenades, rockets, heavy weapons.

However, if you want to deal with people behind cover, it is very simple, flank. Sure, you will probably get shot if you aren’t stealthy, but, eh, ces’t la vie.

Armor: So, armor, like I said during gear, get the expensive stuff, and, if you’re already a little crazy, just get a medium armorjack, the subdermal armor isn’t worth it.

        Now, I’ve fought edgerunners with good armor, and nothing will make others retreat quite like amazing armor. Now I know this, having watched compatriots run the moment they realize autofire is the only way I’m able to damage someone. So it can be extremely helpful.

        But be warned. Rockerboys, and crazier edgerunners will prefer hand cannons and explosives. And while whatever your fighting won’t always penetrate whatever you’re wearing, kinetic force will, and that’s how you (and I did) break a rib.

Fire: Nothing quite like it. Remember, molotov cocktails are finicky, and will not work, incendiary shells and flamethrowers work better.

        When it comes to dealing with fire, however, stay away from fire-spewers. And you’ll probably be fine. Also of note, nades vs fire is entirely dependent on distance. So whoever wins is whoever moves faster.

Melee: The best reason to get a linear frame. So, there’s tons of ways to fight in melee, you could get a big pipe, hopefully a drive shaft like I have.

        In short, this stuff breaks armor better than anything. And can crush bones easily.

NADES!: The bane of all skilled edgreuners. What can I say, nades fix everything. The only thing that really stops them is melee and if you can put down your attacker before they get to you, you’ve got no problems.

        Though, fair warning, and a tip I’ve exploited a number of times. Edgerunners who use nades get cocky. Very, very quickly. So if you, say, pack nades too, ambush, and out damage them. You can kill them with alarming eaze.

Ambushes: Your best friend and your worst nightmare. This is fairly simple, be paranoid to avoid ambushes, and be ruthless to achieve ambushes.

        You will feel guilt, yes, but, ces’t la vie.

Keeping your priorities straight: This is especially important for Solos, you can change what you focus on in a flash. For example, if I’m in combat, I’m entirely focused on shooting straight. If I’m not in combat, I’m entirely paranoid.
